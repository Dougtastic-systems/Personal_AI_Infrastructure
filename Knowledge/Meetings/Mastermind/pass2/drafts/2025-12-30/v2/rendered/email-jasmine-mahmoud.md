## Standout Moment

### Framework: Interrupting Rumination Spirals
*Jasmine Mahmoud* (00:52:00 - 01:15:00)

Jasmine shared a framework she developed for breaking out of unproductive mental loops. The core problem: when we're stuck, we ask ourselves questions like 'How am I going to do this?' or 'What if it doesn't work?' - questions that keep us in a bad state.

**The Framework:**
- Notice when you're in a rumination spiral (anxiety, paralysis, negative self-talk)
- Recognize the unhelpful questions you're asking yourself
- Replace them with: 'What would make today successful?'
- This isn't a productivity hack - it's a nervous system intervention

**Key Insights:**
> "We don't need generic productivity hacks. We actually need nervous system interventions."

> "On the horizon, potentially countering culture of despair... that's what gets me excited."

**How It Landed:**
- **Julie Dorshkind**: This is amazing. I've never heard a framework like that before.
- **Stara Shakti**: This could be a TED talk.
- **Doug Snyder**: Challenged Jasmine to use her own framework and report back.
- **Lizzy Summers**: Immediately applied it: 'I'll figure out one thing I want to do today. Just one thing.'

**Ripple Effect:** Julie noted the power of language: 'What would make today successful?' vs 'What do I need to get done?' creates completely different energy.

## Themes That Emerged
**Nervous System Over Productivity**: We don't need better productivity hacks - we need interventions that regulate our nervous system state first.

**The Power of Better Questions**: The specific words we use in self-inquiry change our state and outcomes. 'What would make today successful?' vs 'What do I need to get done?' creates completely different energy.
