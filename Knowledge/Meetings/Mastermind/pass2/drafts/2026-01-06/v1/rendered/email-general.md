## Standout Moments

### Peer Coaching Principles
*Rick Webster* (00:09:27 - 00:22:00)

Rick laid out principles for being effective peer coaches to each other, emphasizing that we're not mechanics fixing engines or traffic cops punishing mistakes.

**The Framework:**
- Presume competence - it's not your field, they will figure it out
- Emphasize curiosity over interrogation
- Avoid yes/no questions - ask deeper questions that help them process
- Separate the person from the system - 'The system you're using is failing, not you'
- Be aware that everyone carries things behind their smile

**Key Insights:**
> "We don't want to just be giving the advice of, if I were in your shoes, I would do such and such. We all know that doesn't work."

> "You don't know what's behind someone's smile. People are struggling with things that we might not know about."

**How It Landed:**
- **Stara Shakti**: I really love what you said about presuming competence. Even if it's not there right now, that person will get there.
- **Lizzy Summers**: I have to have confidence in ourselves too. I tell myself there's no way I can handle this, but there is a part of me that's going to know.

**Ripple Effect:** Set the tone for how the group would support Lizzy, Stara, and Jasmine's presentations

### Jasmine's Masking Framework
*Jasmine Mahmoud* (00:58:54 - 01:10:00)

Jasmine presented her framework on masking, inspired by Dr. Amen's work on SSRIs and worry. She reframed masking as trying to eliminate error in advance, and proposed unmasking as 'be real and skillful' with capacity for repair.

**The Framework:**
- Healthy worry = conscientiousness (points to values, produces action, lets go)
- Pathological worry = compulsory, keeps running after reasonable steps, trying to buy certainty with thoughts
- Masking = trying to eliminate error in advance, pre-editing everything, expensive and often fails
- Unmasking = not 'be raw and hope for the best' but 'be real and skillful'
- Key insight: You don't need perfect understanding up front, you need capacity to repair misunderstanding

**Key Insights:**
> "Masking is basically trying to eliminate error in advance. You're pre-editing everything, hiding anything risky, aiming for zero misunderstanding."

> "How can I be a voice that can help counter that negativity? On social media it's very much a culture of despair."

**How It Landed:**
- **Rick Webster**: Do you have a goal in mind for these?

**Ripple Effect:** Jasmine shared she got 4000 views on her TikTok video - testing short-form content for her ideas
