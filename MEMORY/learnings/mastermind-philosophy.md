# Mastermind Philosophy: The Change Detection Framework

**Captured:** 2026-01-10
**Context:** Designing fabrication-proof meeting intelligence for mastermind groups

---

## Core Insight

The purpose is NOT to be overly complimentary. It's to show that **their words matter** - helping members become aware of the benefits of being in a group with a desired common trajectory.

Making the space accessible and inviting helps **amplify the signals of change** - honing in on others' micro-changes because they can see it before the person who is changing.

---

## The ADHD 2e Challenge

Self-observation is hard. For ADHD 2e, it's even harder because:

1. **Overdriven somatic system** - often muting subtle signals
2. **Attention primarily activated through firehose phasic dopamine flow and/or cortisol** - over-driving attention to anything BUT the subtle signals of change
3. **Internal narrative** - often reliving past perceived shortcomings or looking for threats and patterns of the past

### The Negative Reward Loop

The attention system has optimized for rewarding early detection of threat - the situation pattern you can map to the thing you're trying to avoid (when life isn't going your way).

This creates a cycle:
- Reward keeps happening for seeking & cementing that pattern further into memory
- Depending on allostatic load, we slip faster out of PFC thinking modes back into Amygdala-based cognition
- This effect is amplified for ADHD 2e
- **We're rewarding ourselves for seeking the negative and then avoiding** - often in any direction, just away from the "bad thing"
- This often leaves us in a more difficult situation

---

## The Solution: Community-Based Change Detection

Use this community model to learn and operationalize by watching someone change from **running away** to finding the beginnings of **running towards**.

### The Motivation Stack
- Curiosity
- Passion
- Purpose
- Autonomy to seek mastery in what you are doing

### The Practice Loop

1. **Watching your peers** - observe change in others
2. **Practicing yourself with intention**
3. **Modeling your practice to your peers** - showing how you did it
4. **A different kind of accountability** - one that supports real change, because change is hard
5. **Supporting by witnessing their change** - be able to listen when others, your team, those who "have your back" are whispering they can see change
6. **Accept their observations** - help maintain momentum until your system response rewards this new place

## The Fractal of Coaching

**Goal:** Practice becoming super learners through the fractals of coaching

### Level 1: Self-Coaching
- Expanding mental models
- Deliberate practice of orienting self
- Understanding the system you are in
- What parts and to what degree you can influence it
- Link them to 1st principles
- Start modifying your environment
- Aligning with your motivation stack

### Level 2: Peer Coaching
- How to ask better questions to help your team gain self clarity
- Knowing when to engage specific group coaching or 1-on-1 coach

### Level 3: Group/1:1 Coaching
- Structured intervention when peer coaching isn't sufficient
